## Purpose

This document records GridPoint Energy's registered functions in the **NERC Compliance Registry** under registration ID **NCR11027**, explains the reliability obligation each function imposes, and establishes the registration-to-standards linkage that determines which CIP Reliability Standards apply to the Company. The functional registration is the legal trigger for mandatory compliance: a Registered Entity is only accountable for standards applicable to the functions for which it is registered.

## Registration Basis

Under Section 215 of the Federal Power Act and the NERC Rules of Procedure, any entity that owns, operates, or uses Bulk Electric System facilities and performs a **registerable function** must be listed in the NERC Compliance Registry. Registration is administered by the Regional Entity — **ReliabilityFirst (RF)** — on behalf of NERC. GridPoint's vertically integrated business model means it performs multiple registerable functions and is therefore registered for **five** functions.

## Registered Functions

GridPoint is registered for the following five functions. Each is defined in the NERC Glossary of Terms and the NERC Functional Model, and each carries a distinct set of reliability obligations.

| Code | Function | What GridPoint owns/operates | Core obligation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GO** | Generator Owner | The ~1,850 MW fleet: Millbrook CC, Easton CC, Cedar Falls Hydro, Sunfield Solar | Owns generation facilities; accountable for asset-based standards including CIP protection of generation BES Cyber Systems |
| **GOP** | Generator Operator | Real-time dispatch of the generating fleet via the Control Centers | Operates generation in real time; must protect Cyber Assets used in generation operations |
| **TO** | Transmission Owner | ~1,600 circuit-miles at 138/345 kV; 44 substations | Owns transmission facilities; accountable for physical and cyber protection of transmission BES Cyber Systems (incl. CIP-014 for critical stations) |
| **TOP** | Transmission Operator | Primary (Millbrook) and Backup (Easton) Control Centers | Operates the transmission system in real time; Control Center BES Cyber Systems categorized Medium impact |
| **DP** | Distribution Provider | Distribution serving ~750,000 customers | Delivers energy to end-use customers; limited CIP applicability, primarily UFLS/UVLS and cyber assets affecting the BES |

### GO — Generator Owner
As GO, GridPoint owns the four generating sites. The GO function makes the Company accountable for the CIP protection of BES Cyber Systems associated with generation. Under GridPoint's CIP-002 categorization, the four generation plants are **Low impact**, so generation carries the Low-impact obligations of CIP-003 Attachment 1 (cyber security awareness, physical security controls, electronic access controls, incident response, and transient/removable-media controls) rather than the full Medium-impact requirement set.

### GOP — Generator Operator
As GOP, GridPoint operates its generating units in real time. The GOP obligations overlap with GO for CIP purposes but focus on operational Cyber Assets (control and dispatch systems). Where generation operations rely on Control Center systems, the higher Medium-impact controls applicable to the Control Centers govern those shared systems.

### TO — Transmission Owner
As TO, GridPoint owns ~1,600 circuit-miles of transmission and 44 substations. This function drives the largest share of CIP obligation: eight 345 kV substations meet CIP-002 Attachment 1 Medium criteria, and the TO function also triggers **CIP-014-3** physical-security obligations for any transmission stations identified as critical through the required risk assessment.

### TOP — Transmission Operator
As TOP, GridPoint operates the transmission system in real time from the Primary (Millbrook) and Backup (Easton) Control Centers. Control Centers performing the TOP function are categorized **Medium impact** under CIP-002 Attachment 1, which brings the full breadth of Medium-impact CIP-004 through CIP-011 and CIP-013 requirements to those facilities.

### DP — Distribution Provider
As DP, GridPoint delivers energy to ~750,000 metered customers. The DP function has the narrowest CIP footprint: applicability is generally limited to Distribution Providers that own certain UFLS/UVLS systems, Remedial Action Schemes, cranking paths/black-start resources, or Protection Systems affecting the BES. GridPoint evaluates these criteria in CIP-002 and treats qualifying assets accordingly.

## Registration-to-Standards Linkage

The registered functions, combined with the CIP-002 impact categorization, determine which standards and requirement parts apply. The linkage flows: **registered function → BES facilities → BES Cyber Systems → CIP-002 impact rating → applicable CIP requirement parts.**

```mermaid
    B --> C[BES Cyber Systems]
    C --> D[CIP-002 Impact Rating<br/>Medium / Low]
    D --> E[Applicable CIP Requirement Parts<br/>118 in scope]
```

| Function | Primary CIP standards engaged |
|---|---|
| GO | CIP-002, CIP-003 (Low Attachment 1), CIP-004–011/013 where Medium assets exist |
| GOP | CIP-002, CIP-003, operational Cyber Asset controls |
| TO | CIP-002, CIP-003–011, CIP-013, **CIP-014-3** (critical stations) |
| TOP | CIP-002 through CIP-011, CIP-013 (Medium Control Centers) |
| DP | CIP-002, CIP-003 (Low), UFLS/UVLS and BES-affecting Protection Systems |

## Joint Registration & Coordination Considerations

Because GridPoint performs five functions under a single legal entity, several standards apply through more than one registration simultaneously. The Company coordinates these overlapping obligations to avoid duplicate or conflicting evidence:

| Consideration | Handling |
|---|---|
| Shared Control Center systems (TOP + GOP) | Categorized once at the higher Medium rating; a single evidence set serves both functions |
| Generation ownership vs. operation (GO + GOP) | Asset-based obligations tracked to GO; operational Cyber Asset controls tracked to GOP |
| Transmission ownership vs. operation (TO + TOP) | Physical/critical-station obligations to TO; real-time control-center obligations to TOP |
| DP overlap with TO facilities | UFLS/UVLS and BES-affecting Protection Systems evaluated once and attributed to the correct function |

This single-entity, multi-function posture is common for a vertically integrated IOU and is why GridPoint's applicable requirement-part count (**118**) spans the full CIP family rather than a narrow slice.

## Functional Model Alignment

The five registrations align to the NERC **Functional Model**, which describes the reliability tasks each function performs. GridPoint's registrations map cleanly to the Functional Model's Reliability Service tasks:

- **GO/GOP** perform the *Generation* reliability tasks — owning and operating resources that inject energy into the BES.
- **TO/TOP** perform the *Transmission* reliability tasks — owning and operating the high-voltage network and its real-time operation.
- **DP** performs the *Distribution* task of delivering energy to end-use load.

Maintaining this alignment ensures that when NERC revises the Functional Model or a standard's Applicability section, GridPoint can quickly confirm whether the change affects one of its registered functions.

## Registry Maintenance Obligations

GridPoint must keep its registry information current with ReliabilityFirst. Material changes — such as the commissioning of **Sunfield Solar (220 MW)**, the addition of new substations, or control-center modernization — must be reflected in the registry and re-evaluated for impact categorization. Deregistration or scope changes follow the NERC Rules of Procedure and require RF concurrence. The NERC Compliance Manager (Karen Whitfield) owns the registry-maintenance process and coordinates any changes with RF.
